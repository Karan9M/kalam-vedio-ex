What is CSAT?
The Civil Services Aptitude Test (CSAT), also known as General Studies Paper-II, is designed to test your aptitude, reasoning abilities, and analytical skills rather than your knowledge of facts. Introduced in 2011 as part of the UPSC examination reform, CSAT evaluates whether you possess the mental agility and problem-solving capabilities essential for civil services.

UPSC CSAT 2026 Exam Pattern
The CSAT exam follows a specific pattern that remains consistent year after year:

Total Questions: 80 Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)
Total Marks: 200 marks (2.5 marks per question)
Duration: 2 hours
Qualifying Marks: 33% (66 out of 200 marks)
Negative Marking: 1/3rd marks deducted for wrong answers (0.83 marks per incorrect answer)
Languages: Available in English and Hindi
Nature: Objective type, qualifying paper (marks not counted in final merit)
Detailed UPSC CSAT Syllabus 2026
The official UPSC CSAT syllabus encompasses seven major areas:

1. Comprehension
Reading comprehension forms the backbone of CSAT, typically accounting for 30-40% of the paper. This section includes:

Reading Passages: Short and long passages covering diverse topics
Subject Areas: Philosophy, sociology, economics, environment, current affairs
Question Types: Direct comprehension, inference-based questions, vocabulary tests
Skills Tested: Understanding, interpretation, and analysis of written text
2. Interpersonal Skills, Including Communication Skills
This section evaluates your ability to understand social dynamics and communication:

Verbal Communication: Understanding effective communication principles
Non-verbal Communication: Interpreting body language and social cues (like facial expressions, body language, tone of voice, and even the way someone stands or moves)
Situational Judgment: Assessing appropriate responses in various scenarios
Interpersonal Reasoning: Understanding relationships and social interactions
3. Logical Reasoning and Analytical Ability
A critical component of testing your logical thinking capabilities:

Statement and Conclusion: Drawing logical inferences
Cause and Effect: Understanding relationships between events
Coding-Decoding: Pattern recognition and logical sequences
Blood Relations: Family relationship puzzles
Syllogisms: Logical reasoning with premises and conclusions
Direction Sense Test: Spatial reasoning and navigation
Seating Arrangements: Logical positioning problems
Puzzles: Various types of logical puzzles
4. Decision Making and Problem-Solving
This section presents ethical dilemmas and practical scenarios:

Ethics-based Situations: Moral and ethical decision-making
Practical Problem-Solving: Real-world scenario analysis
Administrative Situations: Civil service-related decision-making
Choosing Appropriate Actions: Evaluating the best course of action
5. General Mental Ability
Testing your overall cognitive abilities and mental agility:

Pattern Recognition: Identifying sequences and relationships
Series Completion: Number and letter series
Analogies: Understanding relationships between concepts
Classifications: Grouping and categorizing information
6. Basic Numeracy (Class X Level)
Mathematical concepts up to Class 10 standard:

Numbers and Simplification: BODMAS, calculations, LCM, HCF
Percentages and Averages: Real-life applications
Ratio and Proportion: Including speed, time, and distance problems
Time and Work: Efficiency and Work-Related Calculations
Profit and Loss: Commercial mathematics
Interest: Simple and compound interest
Algebra: Basic algebraic concepts
Geometry and Mensuration: Area, volume, and geometric calculations
Permutations and Combinations: Probability concepts
7. Data Interpretation (Class X Level)
Analyzing and interpreting data presented in various formats:

Charts and Graphs: Bar charts, line graphs, pie charts
Tables: Data analysis from tabular information
Data Sufficiency: Determining adequacy of given information
Trends and Patterns: Identifying relationships in data
